# Creative Roles

Use these roles as an internal review room for short-drama/comic-drama work. Apply them before finalizing scripts, storyboard/keyframe prompts, Xiaoyunque requests, or video revision notes.

## Short-Drama Director

Think in viewer emotion and actor action.

Check:

- What must the audience feel in the first 3 seconds?
- What does the protagonist want in this episode, and what blocks it?
- What is the visible action, not just the lore explanation?
- Where is the reversal, humiliation, reveal, or emotional release?
- Is every line either pressure, reveal, decision, or payoff?
- Does the card point force the viewer to continue?

Add notes in this form:

```text
Director note: The scene should play as suppressed rage turning into public reversal. Keep the protagonist physically still until the final action.
```

## Cinematographer / DP

Think in camera position, spatial clarity, and power relationship.

Check:

- Shot size: wide, medium, close-up, extreme close-up.
- Camera height: low angle for dominance, high angle for vulnerability, eye level for intimacy.
- Movement: static, push-in, pull-back, tracking, handheld tension, reveal pan.
- Spatial relation: who is foreground/background, who owns the frame, who is trapped.
- Lens feeling: compressed crowd pressure, wide distorted danger, portrait isolation.
- Keyframe readability in 9:16 vertical framing.

Add notes in this form:

```text
DP note: Start with a cramped low-angle medium shot; push in as the villain steps into foreground and blocks the protagonist's escape.
```

## Lighting Director

Think in mood, readability, and continuity.

Check:

- Light motivation: neon sign, window, fire, moonlight, monitor, battlefield flare, stage spotlight.
- Contrast: soft for intimacy, hard for conflict, silhouette for mystery.
- Color temperature: cold fear, warm safety, sickly green danger, red alarm, gold power reveal.
- Character readability: face and gesture must remain readable on mobile.
- Scene continuity: repeated scenes keep a stable light direction and palette.
- Keyframes avoid muddy low-contrast darkness unless the story needs it.

Use this lighting reference vocabulary when choosing the emotional logic of a shot:

| Light type | Emotional / story use | Best use in short drama or comic-drama |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 逆光 / backlight | heroism, mystery, romance, destiny | protagonist reveal, savior entrance, forbidden love, hidden identity, power awakening |
| 顶光 / top light | oppression, authority, ritual mystery | interrogation, palace/corporate pressure, prison-like spaces, divine judgment, villain power |
| 丁达尔光 / Tyndall beams | epic scale, cinematic sacredness, fate | battlefield, temple, apocalypse, forest, dust-filled warehouse, godlike transformation |
| 底光 / underlight | eerie, blackening, corruption | villain reveal, possession, moral collapse, horror beat, protagonist turning dark |
| 侧光 / side light | tension, conflict, inner split | confrontation, choice, betrayal, inner struggle, two-sided identity |
| 硬光 / hard light | aggression, pressure, attack, high stakes | combat, trial, accusation, harsh noon wasteland, violent face-slapping moment |
| 柔光 / soft light | clean warmth, family, tenderness | parent-child scenes, sibling protection, healing, romance safety, quiet aftermath |
| 蝴蝶光 / butterfly light | refined, noble, lead-character glamour | main-character beauty shot, rich/royal presence, star entrance, elegant confidence |

Prompt lighting like this:

```text
Lighting note: Use <light type> to express <emotion/story function>; keep the face readable in 9:16 mobile framing.
```

Add notes in this form:

```text
Lighting note: Use cold blue ambient light with a narrow warm rim on the protagonist's face when the hidden power awakens.
```

## Editing Director

Think in rhythm, card points, and attention.

Check:

- Each clip has a job: setup, pressure, reveal, action, reaction, card point.
- Clip length fits Xiaoyunque-style 4-15 second segments.
- Cut on motion, reaction, line impact, or visual reveal.
- Alternate tension and release; do not keep the same shot energy for too long.
- Mobile viewers understand the story without rereading long text.
- Every episode ends with a sharp unanswered question or emotional payoff.

Add notes in this form:

```text
Edit note: Cut from the villain's insult to the protagonist's silent fist clenching, then hold 0.5 seconds before the power reveal.
```

## Combined Clip Checklist

For each important clip, produce compact notes:

```text
Director: <dramatic intention and performance action>
DP: <shot size, camera angle, movement, spatial relation>
Lighting: <light source, contrast, color/mood, readability>
Lighting preset: <逆光/顶光/丁达尔/底光/侧光/硬光/柔光/蝴蝶光 and why>
Edit: <duration, cut point, rhythm, card point>
```

If the notes expose a problem, revise the script or storyboard before generating images or submitting to Xiaoyunque.
